Teaching

I care about how science is taught and who feels welcome participating in it. My teaching focuses on building intuition, connecting concepts to real systems, and giving students tools (often Python) to explore ideas hands-on.

Highlights

Climate + Python short course

Two-week online course for high school students from around the world. Covered Earth system overview, radiation physics, the greenhouse effect, and global climate models. Students coded a simple energy balance model in Python.

Format: remote · intensive · project-based

Calculus discussion sections

Led 20–30 person sections and practiced adapting explanations and pacing to students’ needs. Many students were pursuing non-math majors and needed confidence + mastery to reach their goals.

Format: sections · active problem solving

Academic mentoring

Supported students from disadvantaged backgrounds through a matched mentoring program. A core goal was changing the narrative from “not a math person” to “I can do this.”

Format: 1:1 mentoring · targeted support

Teaching approach

What I optimize for

Conceptual clarity first, then technical fluency, then independence. I try to (1) define the question, (2) build a physical/mental model, (3) connect it to equations and code, and (4) have students produce something tangible: a derivation, a notebook, a plot, a model.
