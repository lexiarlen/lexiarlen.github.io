Arctic research vessel campaign (2023)

I spent 7 weeks in the Arctic on a research vessel as part of a broader project studying phytoplankton blooms. Alongside the main science goals, I designed and executed additional experiments focused on sea ice physics and observations.

Links

Polar Plankton campaign blog (Medium) · Project page / NSF grant (Stanford)

What I did

Floe size distribution from drone imagery

Brought an independent experiment to quantify the evolution of the floe size distribution using drone imagery, linking observational products to process-based questions about fracture and floe evolution.

Wave attenuation in sea ice

Deployed wave buoys tethered to sediment traps to study ocean wave attenuation in ice. Designed the deployment configuration and documented field procedures and outcomes.

Field execution

Worked within the constraints of ship operations and environmental conditions, coordinating timing, logistics, and safe deployment/recovery while maintaining data records and experiment notes.

Cascadia nodal seismology fieldwork (2021)

During undergrad, I participated in a seismological field campaign in Oregon. I helped deploy and recover nodal seismometers across the Oregon coastal range and supported field communications and outreach.

Links

Cascadia 2021 blog · Field report (PDF)

What I did

Instrument deployment & recovery

Drove across the Oregon coastal range deploying nodal seismometers for ~2 weeks, then returned for ~1 week to recover them.

Blog management

Managed the campaign blog and documented field progress and experiences for a broader audience.

Field logistics

Worked within a field team—planning routes, coordinating tasks, and adapting to conditions and constraints.
